## Step 4 — Open Codictate via Spotlight

Press `Cmd+Space`, search for **Codictate**, and open it.

## Step 5 — Allow Input Monitoring

Codictate needs this to detect your shortcut while other apps are in focus. Click **Open System Settings** and enable the toggle for Codictate.

<img src="StepByStep/Step12.png" height="320" />

<img src="StepByStep/Step13.png" height="380" />

The app may quit after this — that is expected. Reopen it via Spotlight.

<img src="StepByStep/Step14.png" width="700" />

---

## Step 6 — Allow Accessibility access

Codictate needs this to paste transcribed text into other apps. Click **Open System Settings** and enable **both** Codictate entries.

<img src="StepByStep/Step15.png" height="320" />

<img src="StepByStep/Step16.png" height="380" />

---

## Step 7 — Allow Microphone access

Click **Allow** next to Microphone, then enable the toggle in System Settings.

<img src="StepByStep/Step17.png" height="480" />

<img src="StepByStep/Step18.png" height="380" />

---

## Step 8 — You're ready

Press `Option+Space` anywhere to start recording. Press it again to stop — your words appear wherever your cursor is.

## Troubleshooting — Permissions

If dictation isn't working, check **System Settings → Privacy & Security** and make sure Codictate is enabled under all four:

| Permission | Why it's needed |
|------------|-----------------|
| Files & Folders | Saves temporary recordings |
| Accessibility | Pastes transcribed text into other apps |
| Input Monitoring | Detects your shortcut while other apps are in focus |
| Microphone | Records your voice |

<img src="StepByStep/SystemSettings.png" height="480" />

<img src="StepByStep/SubSections.png" height="480" />

Toggle any missing permission on and relaunch Codictate.
